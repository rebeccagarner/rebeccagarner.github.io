Research CV

My research uses culture-independent molecular genetic techniques to track the diversity of microorganisms in lakes across large spatial and temporal scales.

Contemporary microbial eukaryotic diversity in Canadian lakes

1. Protist diversity and metabolic strategy in lakes at a continental scale

We traced the distributions and environmental drivers of protists and their metabolic strategies in 366 freshwater lakes using 18S rRNA gene amplicon sequencing. Manuscript currently under review.

2. Fungal diversity in freshwater lakes

Following our discovery of a rich diversity of fungi in our 18S rRNA gene data set, we are focusing a separate analysis on this group to bring clarity to their distributions in freshwater lakes.

Reconstructing the microbial past by studying DNA preserved in lake sediments

3. Metagenomics perspective on microbial diversity preserved in lake sediment DNA archives

Paleogenetics is a field of research under intense development, especially as applied to lake sediments. We pioneered a novel application of shotgun-metagenomics to detect preindustrial microbial diversity in lake sediments. Our article describing this method and our discovery of bacterioplankton and viruses preserved in sediment metagenomes is titled Sediment Metagenomes as Time Capsules of Lake Microbiomes and is available here.

4. Long-term effects of eutrophication on lake microeukaryotic communities: Insights from the Experimental Lakes Area

The IISD Experimental Lakes Area is an iconic whole-lake experimentation facility in western Ontario. We are analyzing sediment cores from experimentally nutrient-enriched lakes and unmanipulated reference lakes to track the effects of whole-lake eutrophication on microeukaryotic diversity over the 50-year history of the ELA.

5. Comparative paleogenetics of preindustrial and contemporary microeukaryotic assemblages from Canadian lake sediments

We are comparing modern and preindustrial sediments to assess ~150 years of human impact on microeukaryotic communities in eastern Canadian lakes.

Fieldwork
